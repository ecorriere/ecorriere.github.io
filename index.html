Around the Web

Amazon Web Services (AWS)

During the summer of 2020, I worked as a software engineer intern at Amazon Web Services. I joined the API Gateway team and built database tools to increase service efficiency. My main project included designing and implementing an internal extension of the Jeppetto framework that added features needed for our team. Through this project, I learned about various design patterns and strategies for processing data at scale.

Rev

As a software engineer intern at Rev, I worked as a full stack mobile engineer with an emphasis on audio processing. I became familiar with react native, realm, and native code supporting multiple apps with a combined hundreds of thousands of monthly active users. Some of my accomplishments include decreasing an openGL audio visualization render speed by 950x and implementing in-app automated speech recognition through RevAI's API. Overall, our focus was growth, working to bring in new users and deliver affordable transcription services.

UC Berkeley EECS Department

I am currently an undergraduate student instructor (uGSI/TA) teaching Computer Security in UC Berkeley's EECS department. My responsibilities include leading discussion sections, hosting office hours, designing projects, and writing exam problems. More recently, I have also served as our Disabled Students Program (DSP) lead, working with students and DSP staff to make sure we are providing necessary accommodations to ensure every student has the tools to succeed in our course. Prior to joining CS 161: Computer Security, I was a TA for UC Berkeley's introductory CS class, CS 61A: Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs.
